Overview of Faster Payments Service (FPS)

The Faster Payments Service (FPS) is a UK banking initiative that enables near-instant electronic payments between UK bank accounts. Launched in May 2008, FPS revolutionized UK payments by providing same-day, often near-instantaneous transfers, operating 24/7/365.

Unlike BACS which operates on a 3-day cycle, FPS typically completes transactions within seconds, making it ideal for urgent payments, online banking transfers, mobile payments, and standing orders. The service processes over 3 billion transactions annually with a combined value exceeding £2.8 trillion.

Key Characteristics

Core Features:
  • Speed: Payments typically complete within seconds (maximum 2 hours)
  • Availability: 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year
  • Transaction Limit: Standard limit of £1 million per transaction
  • Irrevocability: Once sent, payments cannot be recalled
  • Settlement: Real-time gross settlement via Bank of England

Payment Types Supported

  • Single Immediate Payments (SIP): One-off payments processed immediately
  • Forward Dated Payments (FDP): Scheduled payments for future dates
  • Standing Orders: Regular recurring payments
  • Bulk Payments: Multiple payments in a single file

Governance & Operation

FPS is operated by Pay.UK, the retail payment authority that also oversees BACS and other UK payment systems. The service is regulated by the Payment Systems Regulator (PSR).

Detailed Steps

1. Payment Initiation

The payer initiates payment through:

  • Online banking portal
  • Mobile banking app
  • Branch counter
  • Telephone banking
  • Third-party payment initiation service (Open Banking)

2. Real-Time Validation

The originating PSP performs immediate checks:

  • Account Status: Ensure account is active
  • Available Balance: Confirm sufficient funds
  • Transaction Limits: Check daily limits
  • Fraud Screening: Real-time fraud detection

3-6. Processing & Delivery

The payment message (ISO 20022 pacs.008) is sent to FPS, routed to the beneficiary PSP, and the account is credited immediately.

7. Confirmation

Both parties receive confirmation. The beneficiary sees funds immediately available.

Settlement

Settlement occurs in real-time through the Bank of England's RTGS system. Each transaction is settled individually, ensuring finality and eliminating settlement risk.

Key Message Types

FPS uses ISO 20022 XML message standards for all payment communications.

Common Rejection Reason Codes

  • AC01: Incorrect account number
  • AC04: Closed account
  • AC06: Blocked account
  • AM04: Insufficient funds
  • AGNT: Incorrect agent (wrong bank)
  • DUPL: Duplicate payment

Message Formats

FPS uses ISO 20022 XML schema for all payment messages.

Example pacs.008 Message (Simplified)

Key Message Elements

  • MsgId: Unique message identifier
  • CreDtTm: Message creation date/time
  • InstrId: Instruction identifier
  • EndToEndId: End-to-end transaction reference
  • IntrBkSttlmAmt: Interbank settlement amount
  • Dbtr/Cdtr: Debtor/Creditor details
  • RmtInf: Remittance information

Security & Compliance

Technical Security Measures

1. Message Security

  • Encryption: TLS 1.3+ for all messages
  • Digital Signatures: ISO 20022 message signing
  • Authentication: PKI infrastructure

2. Fraud Prevention

  • Behavioral analytics and anomaly detection
  • Velocity checks
  • Geo-location analysis
  • Machine learning fraud models
  • Sanctions screening

3. Confirmation of Payee (CoP)

Mandatory for FPS participants since 2020. CoP verifies the beneficiary name matches the account details before payment is sent, reducing misdirected payments and APP fraud.

CoP Process:

  1. Payer enters beneficiary details
  2. Originating PSP sends CoP request
  3. Beneficiary PSP checks name match
  4. Response returned in real-time
  5. Payer receives match result

Regulatory Compliance

UK Regulations

  • Payment Services Regulations 2017: Consumer protection
  • PSR Requirements: Payment Systems Regulator oversight
  • FCA Authorization: Required for participants
  • Money Laundering Regulations: AML/KYC compliance

Data Protection

  • UK GDPR: Personal data handling
  • Data Retention: 6 years minimum
  • ISO 27001: Information security compliance

Consumer Protection

Unauthorized Transactions

Under PSRs 2017:

  • PSP must refund unauthorized payments immediately
  • Customer liability limited to £35
  • PSP liable if failed strong customer authentication

Authorized Push Payment (APP) Fraud

  • PSPs should reimburse victims (no customer negligence)
  • Mandatory CoP checks
  • Enhanced customer warnings

Settlement Process

FPS uses Real-Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) via the Bank of England.

Key Characteristics

  • Gross Settlement: Each payment settled individually
  • Real-Time: Concurrent with payment processing
  • Central Bank Money: Bank of England accounts
  • Finality: Immediate and irrevocable
  • No Credit Risk: Eliminates settlement risk

Settlement Flow

RTGS SETTLEMENT FOR FPS TRANSACTION ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════ Payment: £1,000 BEFORE SETTLEMENT: Originating PSP RTGS Account: £10,000,000 Beneficiary PSP RTGS Account: £15,000,000 SETTLEMENT TRANSACTION: Debit Originating PSP: -£1,000 Credit Beneficiary PSP: +£1,000 AFTER SETTLEMENT: Originating PSP RTGS Account: £9,999,000 Beneficiary PSP RTGS Account: £15,001,000 TIMING: 2-5 seconds for complete settlement

Advantages & Limitations

Advantages of FPS

Key Benefits:
  • Speed: Near-instant (5-20 seconds typically)
  • Availability: 24/7/365 operation
  • Finality: Real-time settlement
  • Convenience: Immediate fund access
  • Certainty: Real-time confirmation
  • Customer Experience: Modern banking

Limitations of FPS

1. Transaction Limits

  • £1 million scheme maximum
  • Individual banks often impose lower limits
  • Online banking limits typically lower

2. Irrevocability

Once sent, payments cannot be recalled. This creates risk for:

  • Accidental payments to wrong accounts
  • Overpayments or duplicates
  • APP fraud

Mitigation: Confirmation of Payee helps reduce misdirected payments.

3. Cost Considerations

  • Higher per-transaction cost than BACS
  • Expensive infrastructure for PSPs
  • Some banks charge fees
