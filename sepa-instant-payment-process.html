Overview of SEPA Instant Payments⚡ 10 Seconds

SEPA Instant Credit Transfer (SCT Inst) is a pan-European payment scheme enabling real-time euro transfers across the Single Euro Payments Area (SEPA). Launched in November 2017, it allows credit transfers to be completed within 10 seconds, 24/7/365, with immediate fund availability to the beneficiary.

SEPA Instant builds upon the successful standard SEPA Credit Transfer (SCT) scheme but adds real-time processing, making it competitive with domestic instant payment systems while maintaining pan-European reach across 36 countries.

Key Characteristics

Core Features:
  • Speed: Maximum 10 seconds end-to-end processing
  • Availability: 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year
  • Transaction Limit: €100,000 per transaction (scheme maximum)
  • Coverage: 36 SEPA countries (EU, EEA, and associated territories)
  • Currency: Euro (EUR) only
  • Irrevocability: Payments cannot be recalled once processed
  • Settlement: Real-time settlement in central bank money

SEPA Coverage

SEPA Instant is available across 36 countries:

  • EU Member States (27): Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Croatia, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Netherlands, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden
  • EEA Countries (3): Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway
  • Other (6): Andorra, Monaco, San Marino, Switzerland, United Kingdom, Vatican City

Governance & Standards

SEPA Instant is governed by the European Payments Council (EPC), which maintains the SCT Inst Rulebook. The scheme uses ISO 20022 XML messaging for all payment instructions and related messages.

Regulatory Landscape

EU Instant Payments Regulation (2024):

The EU has mandated that by early 2025, all payment service providers offering regular SEPA Credit Transfers must also offer SEPA Instant payments at the same price. This regulation aims to make instant payments the new normal across Europe.

  • Mandatory Offering: All PSPs must support SCT Inst receiving by early 2025
  • Price Parity: SCT Inst must cost the same as regular SCT
  • Fraud Screening: Enhanced fraud and sanctions screening required
  • Verification: Beneficiary name verification (similar to CoP)

Clearing & Settlement Mechanisms (CSM)

The Clearing and Settlement Mechanism (CSM) is the infrastructure layer that processes SEPA Instant payments between Payment Service Providers (PSPs). Multiple CSMs operate across Europe, providing the technical platform for instant payment processing.

What is a CSM?

Definition:

A CSM is an automated technical infrastructure that:

  • Receives payment instructions from sending PSPs
  • Validates and processes transactions in real-time
  • Routes payments to receiving PSPs
  • Facilitates settlement between PSPs
  • Ensures 24/7/365 availability
  • Guarantees 10-second processing maximum

Major European CSM Providers

1. TIPS (TARGET Instant Payment Settlement)

Operator: European Central Bank (Eurosystem)

Launch: November 2018

Settlement: Central bank money (TARGET2 accounts)

Key Features:

  • Operated by the Eurosystem (network of EU central banks)
  • Settlement in central bank money (highest level of safety)
  • Integrated with TARGET2 RTGS system
  • No value limit per transaction
  • Participants must have TARGET2 account
  • Primary settlement mechanism for many European banks

Architecture:

  • Dual data centers (primary in Germany, backup in Italy)
  • Direct participant model and reachable party model
  • Real-time liquidity management tools
  • Integration with national RTGS systems

2. RT1 (Real-Time 1)

Operator: EBA CLEARING (The Clearing House of the Euro Banking Association)

Launch: November 2017 (first SCT Inst CSM)

Settlement: Commercial bank money (via settlement banks)

Key Features:

  • First pan-European instant payment infrastructure
  • Settlement through commercial settlement banks
  • No direct central bank account required
  • Extensive European coverage
  • Integrated fraud and sanctions screening
  • Participant can be direct or indirect (via settlement bank)

Settlement Banks:

  • ABN AMRO
  • BBVA
  • BNP Paribas
  • Deutsche Bank
  • HSBC
  • Santander
  • And others

3. CORE (FR)

Operator: STET (French payment infrastructure)

Focus: Primarily French market

Settlement: Integration with Banque de France

Key Features:

  • Serves French banking community
  • Integrated with French national infrastructure
  • Interoperable with other European CSMs
  • Settlement in central bank money via Banque de France

4. National CSMs

Several countries operate national instant payment CSMs:

  • Germany: RPS (Real-Time Payment System) by Deutsche Bundesbank
  • Spain: SNCE (Sistema Nacional de Compensación Electrónica)
  • Italy: BI-COMP operated by Bank of Italy
  • Poland: Express Elixir

CSM Participation Models

1. Direct Participant

  • Direct technical connection to CSM
  • Own settlement arrangement (TIPS account or settlement bank)
  • Full control over payment processing
  • Typically large banks

2. Indirect Participant (Reachable Party)

  • Reaches CSM through a direct participant
  • Direct participant handles settlement
  • Lower entry costs
  • Typically smaller PSPs, fintechs

SEPA Instant payments operate on a near-real-time basis with a strict 10-second maximum processing time. The entire journey from initiation to beneficiary credit must complete within this window.

Detailed Step-by-Step Process

1. Payment Initiation (< 1 second)

Originator initiates payment through:

  • Mobile banking app
  • Online banking
  • QR code payment
  • Request-to-Pay (RTP) service
  • API/third-party payment initiation

2. PSP Validation (< 1 second)

Originating PSP performs real-time checks:

  • Account Status: Active and not blocked
  • Available Balance: Sufficient funds (€100,000 limit)
  • Transaction Limits: Daily/per-transaction limits
  • Fraud Screening: AML, sanctions, pattern analysis
  • Beneficiary Verification: Name matching (if supported)
  • Liquidity Check: Settlement account has funds

3. Message Submission (< 1 second)

Originating PSP constructs ISO 20022 pacs.008 message and submits to CSM with all payment details.

4. CSM Processing (< 3 seconds)

CSM performs:

  • Message format validation
  • Duplicate detection
  • Routing to beneficiary PSP (including cross-CSM routing)
  • Sanctions screening
  • Transaction logging

5. Forward to Beneficiary PSP (< 1 second)

Payment delivered to receiving PSP for processing.

6. Beneficiary PSP Processing (< 2 seconds)

Receiving PSP:

  • Validates beneficiary account exists and is active
  • Performs AML/sanctions checks
  • Credits beneficiary account immediately
  • Makes funds available instantly
  • Generates response message

7-9. Response & Confirmation (< 2 seconds)

pacs.002 response travels back through CSM to originating PSP, confirming acceptance or rejection. Customer receives confirmation.

Settlement (Parallel Process)

Settlement occurs in real-time via CSM settlement mechanism (TIPS or settlement banks), running parallel to message processing.

Rejection Scenarios

Common Rejection Reasons:

  • AC01: Incorrect account number
  • AC04: Closed account
  • AC06: Blocked account
  • AM04: Insufficient funds (originating PSP)
  • AM18: Amount exceeds agreed limit (€100,000)
  • AGNT: Incorrect agent/BIC
  • CUST: Requested by customer
  • DUPL: Duplicate payment
  • NARR: Narrative reason (see additional info)

Rejection Handling:

REJECTION FLOW: ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ 1. Beneficiary PSP detects issue (e.g., closed account) 2. Sends RJCT status in pacs.002 with reason code 3. CSM routes rejection back to originating PSP 4. Originating PSP reverses debit to customer 5. Customer notified of rejection with reason 6. No settlement occurs (payment not completed) Total Time: Still within 10 seconds Result: No funds transferred, customer account unchanged

Key Message Types

SEPA Instant uses ISO 20022 XML messaging exclusively. The scheme defines specific message types for payment initiation, responses, and exceptions.

Core ISO 20022 Message Types

Message Type Name Direction Purpose
pain.001 CustomerCreditTransferInitiation Customer → PSP Payment instruction from customer
pacs.008 FIToFICustomerCreditTransfer PSP → CSM → PSP Interbank payment instruction
pacs.002 FIToFIPaymentStatusReport PSP → CSM → PSP Payment acceptance/rejection
pacs.004 PaymentReturn PSP → CSM → PSP Return of credited payment
pacs.028 FIToFIPaymentStatusRequest PSP → CSM Query payment status
camt.056 FIToFIPaymentCancellationRequest PSP → CSM → PSP Request to cancel payment
camt.029 ResolutionOfInvestigation PSP → CSM → PSP Response to cancellation request

Payment Status Codes

Code Status Description
ACSC AcceptedSettlementCompleted Payment successfully completed and settled
ACSP AcceptedSettlementInProcess Payment accepted, settlement pending
RJCT Rejected Payment rejected (see reason code)
PDNG Pending Payment pending (should not occur in SCT Inst)

Reason Codes (for Rejections)

Most Common Reason Codes:
  • AC01: Incorrect account number format
  • AC04: Closed account number
  • AC06: Blocked account
  • AG01: Transaction forbidden on this type of account
  • AG02: Invalid bank operation code
  • AM04: Insufficient funds
  • AM18: Amount exceeds agreed limit
  • BE01: Inconsistent with end customer
  • CURR: Incorrect currency (must be EUR)
  • CUST: Requested by customer
  • DUPL: Payment is a duplicate
  • FF01: Invalid file format or syntax
  • FOCR: Following cancellation request
  • MS03: Not specified reason agent generated
  • NARR: Narrative (free text explanation required)

Timing & Processing Windows

One of SEPA Instant's key advantages is its 24/7/365 availability with no processing windows or cut-off times.

Service Availability

Operational Hours:
  • Availability: 24/7/365 - no downtime for weekends or holidays
  • Maintenance Windows: Minimal scheduled maintenance (announced in advance)
  • Processing Time: Maximum 10 seconds, typically 3-7 seconds
  • Cut-Off Times: None - payments processed continuously
  • Weekends: Full service
  • Bank Holidays: Full service (all European countries)

Peak Processing Times

While SEPA Instant operates 24/7, certain patterns emerge:

  • Business Hours (09:00-17:00 CET): Highest volume
  • Evenings (18:00-22:00 CET): Consumer peer-to-peer payments
  • Weekends: Moderate volume, primarily consumer transactions
  • Nights (00:00-06:00 CET): Lowest volume
  • Month End: Increased business payment activity

No Cut-Off Time Benefit

Critical Advantage:

Unlike CHAPS (18:00 cut-off) or standard SEPA (daily batch windows), SEPA Instant has no cut-off times. This means:

  • Payments can be made anytime, including weekends and holidays
  • No rush before end-of-day deadlines
  • True on-demand payment capability
  • Ideal for emergency or time-critical payments
  • Supports modern 24/7 business models

Day-to-Day Operations

For Payment Service Providers (PSPs)

1. 24/7 Operations

Continuous Monitoring:
  • Round-the-clock NOC (Network Operations Center)
  • Real-time transaction monitoring
  • Automated alerting for system issues
  • Performance dashboard tracking
  • Liquidity position monitoring (TIPS/settlement accounts)

2. Transaction Processing

Incoming Payments:

  • Receive pacs.008 from CSM
  • Validate beneficiary IBAN
  • Perform AML/sanctions screening (within 10s limit)
  • Credit beneficiary account immediately
  • Send pacs.002 response
  • Notify beneficiary via app/SMS

Outgoing Payments:

  • Receive customer instruction
  • Validate account and balance
  • Check against €100,000 limit
  • Perform fraud screening
  • Check settlement liquidity
  • Submit pacs.008 to CSM
  • Monitor for response

3. Liquidity Management

TIPS Participants:

  • Monitor TIPS account balance continuously
  • Ensure sufficient funds for outgoing payments
  • Use ECB's liquidity management tools
  • Forecast daily payment flows
  • Maintain minimum buffer for peaks

RT1 Participants:

  • Maintain prefunded balances with settlement banks
  • Monitor position in real-time
  • Top-up when approaching limits
  • Coordinate with settlement bank

4. Exception Handling

Exception Action Timeline

Insufficient Liquidity Add funds to settlement account or reject Immediate

Security & Compliance

Security Measures

1. Message Security

  • Encryption: TLS 1.3+ for all CSM communications
  • Digital Signatures: ISO 20022 message signing
  • Authentication: Strong authentication for PSP access
  • Certificate Management: PKI infrastructure

2. Fraud Prevention

Real-Time Fraud Screening:
  • Behavioral analytics (unusual patterns)
  • Velocity checks (frequency and amounts)
  • Device fingerprinting
  • Geolocation analysis
  • Machine learning fraud models
  • Sanctions and PEP screening
  • All within the 10-second window!

3. Beneficiary Verification

Verification of Payee (similar to UK's CoP):

  • EU regulation requires name verification by 2025
  • PSPs must check beneficiary name matches IBAN
  • Real-time response (match, close match, no match)
  • Reduces misdirected payments and fraud

Regulatory Framework

1. EU Regulations

  • PSD2: Strong customer authentication (SCA) required
  • Instant Payments Regulation (2024): Mandatory offering, price parity
  • AML Directives: Anti-money laundering compliance
  • GDPR: Data protection for payment data
  • SEPA Regulation: Harmonized payment rules

2. SCT Inst Scheme Rules

Key Rulebook Requirements:
  • 10-second maximum processing time
  • 24/7/365 availability (99.9%+ uptime)
  • €100,000 transaction limit
  • ISO 20022 messaging mandatory
  • Immediate fund availability to beneficiary
  • Irrevocability of accepted payments
  • Enhanced fraud and sanctions screening

3. Strong Customer Authentication (SCA)

Under PSD2, payment initiation requires SCA:

  • Two-factor authentication: Something you know + something you have
  • Biometric options: Fingerprint, face recognition
  • Mobile app authentication: Push notifications with approval
  • Exemptions: Trusted beneficiaries, low-value payments (under limits)

Consumer Protection

Unauthorized Payments:

  • PSP must refund unauthorized payments immediately
  • Consumer liability limited to €50 (if promptly reported)
  • Zero liability if PSP failed to implement SCA
  • 13-month claim period for unauthorized payments

Authorized Push Payment Fraud:

Challenge:

As with UK's APP fraud, SEPA Instant's irrevocability creates risk when consumers are tricked into authorizing fraudulent payments.

Mitigations:

  • Mandatory beneficiary name verification (from 2025)
  • Enhanced customer warnings for high-risk transactions
  • Real-time fraud screening by PSPs
  • Consumer education campaigns
  • Coordinated fraud databases across PSPs

Data Protection & Privacy

  • GDPR Compliance: Payment data processed lawfully
  • Data Minimization: Only necessary data collected
  • Retention Limits: Payment data retained per regulatory requirements
  • Right to Access: Consumers can request their payment data
  • Breach Notification: Must report data breaches within 72 hours

Settlement Process

SEPA Instant uses real-time settlement, with the specific mechanism depending on the CSM used.

TIPS Settlement Model

TARGET Instant Payment Settlement (TIPS)

TIPS provides real-time gross settlement in central bank money, offering the highest level of safety and finality.

Settlement Flow in TIPS:

TIPS REAL-TIME SETTLEMENT ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ 1. PSP A submits payment of €5,000 to TIPS 2. TIPS checks PSP A's TIPS account balance Current Balance: €10,000,000 Required: €5,000 Status: SUFFICIENT ✓ 3. TIPS executes settlement (simultaneous): PSP A TIPS Account: PSP B TIPS Account: Before: €10,000,000 Before: €15,000,000 Settlement Transaction: Debit PSP A: -€5,000 Credit PSP B: +€5,000 After: €9,995,000 After: €15,005,000 4. Settlement confirmed to both PSPs 5. Payment message delivered to PSP B 6. PSP B credits customer account TIMING: Settlement completes in 1-3 seconds FINALITY: Immediate and irrevocable SAFETY: Central bank money (Eurosystem)

TIPS Account Management:

  • Opening Balance: PSPs fund TIPS accounts at start of day
  • Intraday Management: Monitor balances continuously
  • Liquidity Transfers: Can transfer from TARGET2 to TIPS instantly
  • Automated Rebalancing: Set rules for automatic top-ups
  • Minimum Balance: Maintain buffer for peak periods

RT1 Settlement Model

RT1 (Real-Time 1) by EBA CLEARING

RT1 uses settlement through commercial settlement banks, with subsequent final settlement in TARGET2.

RT1 Settlement Flow:

RT1 SETTLEMENT MODEL ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ PARTICIPANT LEVEL: PSP A → Settlement Bank X (prefunded account) PSP B → Settlement Bank Y (prefunded account) TRANSACTION: €5,000 from PSP A to PSP B Step 1: RT1 processes payment immediately PSP B customer credited instantly Step 2: Settlement Bank level: Bank X owes Bank Y: €5,000 Settled through: • Prefunded balances with each other, OR • Subsequent batch settlement via TARGET2 PARTICIPANT VIEW: PSP A prefunded account: Reduced by €5,000 immediately PSP B prefunded account: Increased by €5,000 immediately FINAL SETTLEMENT: Between settlement banks in TARGET2 (batch or real-time)

Liquidity Management Strategies

For TIPS Participants:

Best Practices:
  • Forecasting: Predict daily instant payment volumes
  • Opening Balance: Fund TIPS account adequately at 07:00 CET
  • Real-Time Monitoring: Track TIPS balance continuously
  • Auto-Replenishment: Set up automatic transfers from TARGET2
  • Contingency Buffer: Maintain 20-30% buffer above expected peak
  • Recycling: Use incoming instant payments to fund outgoing

For RT1 Participants:

  • Maintain prefunded balances with settlement bank
  • Monitor account position in real-time
  • Coordinate with settlement bank for top-ups
  • Understand settlement bank's fee structure
  • Have backup settlement bank relationship

Settlement Failures

Insufficient Liquidity Scenario:

LIQUIDITY FAILURE HANDLING: ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ 1. PSP A submits payment for €50,000 2. TIPS checks balance: €30,000 available 3. INSUFFICIENT FUNDS - payment cannot be settled 4. TIPS rejects payment immediately 5. PSP A receives rejection (within 10 seconds) 6. PSP A must either: a) Add liquidity to TIPS account, then resubmit b) Reject customer payment with "insufficient liquidity" 7. Customer informed payment failed CRITICAL: PSPs must manage liquidity to avoid rejections

Prevention Measures:

  • Pre-check settlement account balance before accepting payment
  • Set internal limits below actual settlement account balance
  • Automated alerts when balance falls below threshold
  • Automated liquidity transfers when needed
  • Reject customer payments if settlement liquidity insufficient

Advantages & Limitations

Advantages of SEPA Instant

Key Benefits:
  • Speed: Maximum 10 seconds (typically 3-7 seconds)
  • 24/7/365 Availability: No weekends, no holidays, no cut-offs
  • Pan-European: Works across 36 SEPA countries
  • Immediate Availability: Funds usable instantly by beneficiary
  • Cost-Effective: Price parity with standard SEPA (EU regulation)
  • Certainty: Real-time confirmation of success/failure
  • Modern Infrastructure: ISO 20022 messaging
  • Central Bank Settlement: Available via TIPS
  • Improved Cash Flow: Instant liquidity for businesses
  • Customer Experience: Modern, instant payment expectation

Limitations of SEPA Instant

1. Transaction Limit

  • €100,000 Maximum: Scheme-level limit per transaction
  • Individual PSP Limits: Many banks impose lower limits
  • Consumer Limits: Often €15,000 or less for retail customers
  • Not Suitable: For very high-value payments (use TARGET2/standard SEPA)

2. Irrevocability

Critical Limitation:

Once processed, SEPA Instant payments cannot be recalled. This creates risks:

  • Payments to wrong beneficiary cannot be reversed
  • Typing errors in IBAN are immediately costly
  • Fraud victims cannot stop payment after authorization
  • Duplicate payments cannot be cancelled
  • Recovery requires beneficiary cooperation

Mitigation: Beneficiary name verification (mandatory from 2025) helps reduce errors.

3. Coverage Gaps

  • Not All PSPs Participate: Some banks don't offer instant receiving/sending
  • Becoming Mandatory: EU regulation fixes this by 2025
  • Non-SEPA Countries: Cannot use SEPA Instant (e.g., USA, UK post-Brexit for some banks)

4. Operational Complexity

  • 24/7 Operations: Expensive for smaller PSPs
  • Liquidity Management: Complex real-time liquidity required
  • Fraud Screening: Must complete within 10 seconds
  • System Availability: 99.9%+ uptime requirement
