Overview of CHAPSHigh-Value

CHAPS (Clearing House Automated Payment System) is the UK's primary high-value same-day payment system for sterling transactions. Operated by the Bank of England since November 2017, CHAPS provides guaranteed same-day settlement for time-critical and high-value payments.

Unlike BACS (3-day cycle) and FPS (near-instant but limited to Â£1M), CHAPS is specifically designed for high-value, time-critical payments with no transaction limit. It processes approximately 35 million transactions annually, with a daily average value exceeding Â£350 billion.

Key Characteristics

Core Features:
  • Settlement: Guaranteed same-day settlement (if submitted by cut-off)
  • Transaction Limit: No maximum transaction limit
  • Finality: Real-time gross settlement - immediate and irrevocable
  • Availability: Business days only (Monday-Friday, excluding bank holidays)
  • Operating Hours: 06:00 - 18:00 (with extended hours available)
  • Cost: Typically Â£25-Â£35 per transaction

Primary Use Cases

  • Property Transactions: House purchases and commercial property deals
  • High-Value Business Payments: Large supplier payments, acquisitions
  • Financial Market Settlement: Securities, foreign exchange, derivatives
  • Interbank Transfers: Bank-to-bank liquidity management
  • Time-Critical Payments: Urgent payments requiring same-day completion

Governance & Operation

CHAPS is owned and operated by the Bank of England, which took over from the private sector in 2017. The system is part of the UK's critical national infrastructure and operates under strict oversight from the Bank's Financial Market Infrastructure Directorate.

Historical Context

CHAPS was established in 1984 to provide same-day sterling payment services. It has undergone several major upgrades:

  • 1984: Launch of CHAPS Clearing Company
  • 1996: Real-time gross settlement introduced
  • 2008: Integration with RTGS system
  • 2017: Bank of England assumes ownership and operation
  • 2024: Transition to ISO 20022 messaging standard

Process Flow

CHAPS operates on a same-day settlement model with real-time gross settlement (RTGS). Each payment is processed individually and settled immediately in central bank money, providing certainty and finality.

Detailed Step-by-Step Process

1. Payment Initiation

The originator initiates a CHAPS payment through:

  • Branch: In-person instruction at bank branch
  • Telephone Banking: Authorized phone instruction
  • Online Banking: For pre-authorized beneficiaries
  • Corporate Banking Platform: Direct submission by corporate treasury
  • Solicitor/Conveyancer: For property transactions

2. Validation & Authorization

The sending bank performs comprehensive checks:

  • Customer Authorization: Verify instruction is genuine and authorized
  • Account Verification: Confirm sufficient funds available
  • Beneficiary Details: Validate sort code, account number, beneficiary name
  • Sanctions Screening: Check against sanctions lists
  • Fraud Checks: High-value transaction screening
  • Reference Information: Ensure complete payment details

3. Submission to CHAPS

The sending bank constructs an ISO 20022 pacs.008 message and submits it to the CHAPS system. The message includes all payment details, settlement instructions, and remittance information.

4. RTGS Settlement

This is the critical step where actual settlement occurs:

  • Bank of England's RTGS system validates the message
  • Checks sending bank has sufficient liquidity in RTGS account
  • Immediately debits sending bank's RTGS account
  • Simultaneously credits receiving bank's RTGS account
  • Settlement is final and irrevocable

5. Delivery to Receiving Bank

Once settled, the payment message is delivered to the receiving bank for customer crediting.

6. Beneficiary Account Credit

The receiving bank:

  • Receives the settled payment notification
  • Validates beneficiary account details
  • Credits beneficiary's account
  • Makes funds immediately available

7. Confirmation Back to Sender

The receiving bank sends a pacs.002 status report confirming receipt and crediting.

8. Customer Notification

The sending bank notifies the originator that the payment has been successfully completed.

Timing & Processing Windows

CHAPS operates during business days only with specific opening hours and cut-off times that are critical for same-day settlement.

Service Availability

Operational Hours:
  • Operating Days: Monday to Friday (excluding UK bank holidays)
  • Core Hours: 06:00 - 18:00 GMT/BST
  • Extended Hours: Available from 20:00 previous day for specific operations
  • Weekends: System closed
  • Bank Holidays: System closed

Same-Day Settlement Requirements

To guarantee same-day settlement:

  1. Submit instruction before bank's customer cut-off (typically 15:00-16:00)
  2. Ensure sending bank has sufficient RTGS liquidity
  3. Provide complete and accurate beneficiary details
  4. Allow for bank processing time (typically 30-60 minutes)
  5. Be aware that late payments may be held until next business day

Bank Holidays & Extended Weekends

CHAPS does not operate on UK bank holidays, which can create extended settlement periods:

  • Friday to Monday: 3-day gap in normal weekends
  • Bank Holiday Weekends: Up to 4-day gaps
  • Christmas/New Year: Potentially 5+ day gaps
Planning Consideration: Property completions and time-critical payments must account for bank holidays and weekends. Always verify next available CHAPS processing day.

  • Review overnight positions and forecasts
  • Ensure adequate RTGS account balances
  • Prepare for system opening
  • Review queued payments from previous day

Continuous Activities:
  • Receive and validate customer payment instructions
  • Construct and submit pacs.008 messages
  • Monitor incoming CHAPS payments
  • Credit customer accounts immediately upon receipt
  • Send confirmations to customers
  • Monitor RTGS liquidity position
  • Handle exceptions and queries

3. End-of-Day Procedures (17:30-19:00)

  • Reconcile all sent and received payments
  • Generate daily reports
  • Investigate any failed or pending payments
  • Prepare next-day forecasts
  • Update customer accounts

Security & Compliance

Security Measures

1. System Security

  • Encryption: End-to-end encryption for all messages
  • Authentication: Strong multi-factor authentication for participants
  • Digital Signatures: All messages digitally signed
  • Dedicated Network: Secure closed network (not internet-based)
  • PKI Infrastructure: Public key infrastructure for identity verification

2. Participant Security Requirements

Banks Must Implement:
  • Secure physical facilities with access controls
  • Segregation of duties for payment operations
  • Maker-checker controls for high-value payments
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Incident response procedures
  • Business continuity and disaster recovery plans

3. Transaction-Level Security

  • Sanctions Screening: All parties checked against sanctions lists
  • AML Checks: Anti-money laundering verification
  • Fraud Detection: Pattern analysis for suspicious activity
  • Confirmation of Payee: Name verification where applicable
  • Audit Trail: Complete logging of all transactions

As operator of CHAPS, the Bank of England:

  • Monitors system performance and resilience
  • Conducts regular participant reviews
  • Manages system changes and upgrades
  • Ensures compliance with international standards

2. Regulatory Framework

  • Payment Services Regulations 2017: Consumer protection framework
  • Financial Services and Markets Act: Regulatory authority
  • CPMI-IOSCO Principles: International standards for FMIs
  • Data Protection: UK GDPR compliance
  • PSD2: Strong customer authentication requirements

Settlement Process

CHAPS uses Real-Time Gross Settlement (RTGS), meaning each payment is settled individually and immediately in central bank money, providing complete finality.

RTGS Settlement Mechanism

Key Characteristics:

  • Gross Settlement: Each payment settled separately (not netted)
  • Real-Time: Settlement occurs immediately upon processing
  • Central Bank Money: Settlements in Bank of England RTGS accounts
  • Finality: Once settled, payment is final and irrevocable
  • Zero Settlement Risk: No credit exposure between banks

Advantages & Limitations

Advantages of CHAPS

Key Benefits:
  • No Transaction Limit: Can process payments of any value
  • Same-Day Settlement: Guaranteed if submitted before cut-off
  • Finality: Immediate and irrevocable settlement
  • Zero Settlement Risk: Real-time gross settlement eliminates credit risk
  • Certainty: Known settlement time for time-critical transactions
  • Central Bank Money: Settled in safest form of money
  • Regulatory Oversight: Operated by Bank of England
  • Proven System: 40+ years of reliable operation

Limitations of CHAPS

1. Cost

  • High Transaction Fees: Typically Â£25-Â£35 per payment
  • Not Cost-Effective: For low-value or routine payments
  • Infrastructure Costs: Expensive for banks to participate

2. Operating Hours

Limited Availability:

  • Business days only (no weekends or bank holidays)
  • Operating hours: 06:00-18:00
  • Customer cut-offs typically 15:00-16:00
  • Extended non-processing periods over long weekends
  • Not suitable for 24/7 payment needs

3. Complexity

  • Requires Expertise: Complex system requiring trained staff
  • Manual Processes: Often requires branch visit or phone call
  • Documentation: May require supporting documentation
  • Cut-Off Management: Must carefully manage timing

4. Irrevocability

  • Once settled, cannot be recalled
  • Errors are difficult and expensive to rectify
  • Recovery requires beneficiary cooperation
  • Legal action may be necessary
